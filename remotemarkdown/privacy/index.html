RemoteMarkdown Privacy Policy

Effective Date: July 16, 2026

This Privacy Policy applies specifically to the RemoteMarkdown mobile application (the “Application”), operated by UksCompany (욱스컴퍼니) (the “Service Provider”). RemoteMarkdown lets users open, view, create, edit, search, and manage text, Markdown, and supported image files stored on their device or in a workspace selected by the user.

RemoteMarkdown does not provide a UksCompany user account and the Service Provider does not operate a server that receives users' document contents or remote-server credentials.

If you have privacy-related questions, contact us at ukscompany24@gmail.com.

Application: RemoteMarkdown

2. Files, Workspaces, and Credentials

2.1 Files selected by the user

The Application accesses files only when the user opens a document, selects a folder or document provider, registers a workspace, or performs a file operation. Depending on the user's selection, files may be stored:

  • on the user's device;
  • through an Android document provider, which may include a service such as Google Drive, OneDrive, or Dropbox; or
  • on a server designated by the user through SMB, SFTP, WebDAV, FTP, or FTPS.

Document contents, file names, file paths, server addresses, account names, credentials, and search terms are not sent to UksCompany's servers. When the user chooses a third-party storage provider or a remote server, the Application necessarily sends requests and file data to that destination to perform the user's requested operation. The destination may receive the user's IP address and is governed by its own privacy and retention practices.

2.2 Workspace information stored on the device

To reconnect to a saved workspace, the Application stores applicable connection metadata in its local app storage. This may include a workspace name or alias, local folder or document-provider information, server address, port, share name, base path, user name, domain, connection type, and an SFTP host-key fingerprint.

Passwords, SSH private keys, and private-key passphrases are stored separately using the operating system's secure storage. On Android, the secure-storage encryption key is protected through Android Keystore when supported by the device. These secrets are used only to connect to the workspace designated by the user and are not sent to UksCompany.

2.3 Local recovery copies and preferences

To reduce the risk of losing edits, the Application may keep one app-private recovery record on the device when changes have not yet been safely written, including when a save fails or the Application moves to the background. A recovery record may contain the workspace identifier, document path, unsaved document contents, encoding, timestamps, and limited document metadata. It is removed after a successful save, when the user discards the recovery copy, or when the Application is uninstalled.

The Application also stores preferences and operational state on the device, such as language, editor settings, keyboard shortcuts, the last workspace or document, review-prompt eligibility, and the last verified advertising-removal entitlement.

3. Google Analytics for Firebase

The Android version of the Application uses Google Analytics for Firebase to understand feature usage and improve the Application. Google Analytics for Firebase may automatically process information such as IP address, approximate geographic region, app-instance and device identifiers (which may include the Android advertising ID), device model, operating system, app version, language, first launch, app opens and updates, sessions, screen views, engagement, and in-app-purchase events.

The Application logs limited custom events concerning:

  • screen views and feature usage;
  • workspace type and whether a connection attempt succeeds or fails;
  • broad document type and whether opening or saving succeeds or fails;
  • view mode, setting changes, and the number of search results;
  • file operations, edit recovery actions, and advertising-removal purchase-flow outcomes.

Custom analytics events do not include document contents, file names, file paths, server addresses, user names, passwords, SSH keys, passphrases, or search terms. The Application does not set a separate UksCompany account identifier in Google Analytics.

4. Advertising

The free version of the Android Application displays banner and interstitial ads provided by Google AdMob. The Google Mobile Ads SDK may automatically collect and share data such as IP address, general location inferred from IP address, advertising ID, app-set ID and other device or account identifiers, ad and app interactions, and diagnostic or performance information. Google may use this data to provide and measure ads, perform analytics, and prevent fraud and abuse.

Ad personalization and related privacy choices depend on the user's region, device settings, and choices presented through Google's consent tools. Users may also reset or delete the Android advertising ID through Android settings. When the Application confirms that the user owns the advertising-removal purchase, the Application does not request banner or interstitial ads.

5. Google Play Purchases and Reviews

Advertising removal is offered as a one-time, non-consumable purchase through Google Play. Google processes the payment method and payment transaction. UksCompany does not receive or store the user's full payment-card or bank-account details. The Application receives the product information and purchase status needed to complete, restore, and locally cache the advertising-removal entitlement.

The Application may also ask eligible users to submit a review using Google Play's in-app review service. Submitting a review is voluntary and is governed by Google Play's terms and privacy practices.

6. Third Parties and Disclosure

The Application uses or communicates with the following parties:

  • Google Privacy Policy — Google Play, purchases, in-app reviews, and related platform services;
  • Privacy and Security in Firebase — Google Analytics for Firebase;
  • Google Advertising Policies — Google AdMob;
  • user-selected document providers and remote servers — local or cloud file access and remote-workspace operations.

UksCompany does not sell users' personal or sensitive data. Data may be disclosed where required by applicable law, legal process, or a valid governmental request, or where reasonably necessary to protect rights, safety, security, and prevent fraud. A user-directed transfer to a selected storage provider or remote server is performed only to provide the requested file-management functionality.

7. Network Security

Data sent by Google Analytics for Firebase and the Google Mobile Ads SDK is encrypted in transit by Google. Passwords, SSH private keys, and passphrases saved by the Application are protected using the operating system's secure storage as described above.

Security between the Application and a user-selected server depends on the protocol and server configuration chosen by the user. SFTP, FTPS, and HTTPS WebDAV provide encrypted transport when correctly configured. Plain FTP and HTTP WebDAV do not encrypt file contents or credentials in transit. SMB transport protection depends on the server and protocol configuration. The Application warns before connecting to certain cleartext public-server configurations, and users should prefer encrypted protocols, trusted servers, and trusted networks.

8. Retention and Deletion

  • Saved workspace metadata and credentials remain on the device until the user removes that workspace or uninstalls the Application.
  • Local preferences and operational state remain until replaced, cleared through the applicable feature, or removed by uninstalling the Application.
  • A local recovery record remains only until a successful save, the user's decision to discard it, or uninstallation.
  • Uninstalling the Application removes its local app data through the operating system, but does not delete files stored on a user's NAS, remote server, device folder, or cloud-storage account.
  • Information already processed by Google is retained and deleted in accordance with Google's policies and the Service Provider's applicable Google service settings.

RemoteMarkdown does not offer or require a UksCompany account. For questions about local-data deletion or data handled by the Service Provider, contact ukscompany24@gmail.com.

9. User Choices

Users may:

  • remove an individual saved workspace from the Application;
  • save or discard a recovery copy when prompted;
  • manage advertising privacy choices through controls made available by Google or through Android settings;
  • stop future collection by the Application and its integrated SDKs by uninstalling the Application.

10. Children's Privacy

RemoteMarkdown is not directed to children under 13, and UksCompany does not knowingly solicit personal information from children under 13 or market the Application to them. If you are a parent or guardian and believe a child has provided personal information to UksCompany, contact ukscompany24@gmail.com so that appropriate action can be taken.
